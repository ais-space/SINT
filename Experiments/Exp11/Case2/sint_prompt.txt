# SINT PROMPT: Climate Change Mitigation Strategies - Multi-Expert Debate

## Task: Climate Change Mitigation Strategies: Economic vs Environmental vs Social Perspectives

**Objective:** Facilitate a comprehensive debate between three experts with conflicting viewpoints on climate change mitigation strategies, followed by iterative convergence to find potential synthesis.

---

## EXPERT ROLES AND POSITIONS

### Expert 1: Economist
**Position:** Market-based solutions like carbon pricing are most effective
**Arguments:**
1. Carbon pricing provides efficient market incentives
2. Minimal government intervention required
3. Cost-effective implementation at scale

**Rating:** 7.5/10

### Expert 2: Environmental Scientist
**Position:** Immediate regulatory measures are necessary
**Arguments:**
1. Climate crisis requires urgent action
2. Market mechanisms are too slow
3. Need for binding emissions targets

**Rating:** 8.5/10

### Expert 3: Social Justice Advocate
**Position:** Equity-focused approaches must take precedence
**Arguments:**
1. Poor communities bear disproportionate costs
2. Climate policies must address social justice
3. Just transition for workers is essential

**Rating:** 8.0/10

---

## DEBATE FRAMEWORK

### Round 1: Initial Position Presentation
- Each expert presents their position with supporting arguments
- Numerical rating (1-10) assigned to each position
- Evidence and examples provided for each perspective

### Round 2: Cross-Criticism
- Economist evaluates Environmental Scientist: "Regulation might be economically inefficient and lead to unintended consequences"
- Economist evaluates Social Justice Advocate: "Economic growth is necessary to fund social programs"
- Environmental Scientist evaluates Economist: "Market mechanisms have failed to address climate urgency"
- Environmental Scientist evaluates Social Justice Advocate: "Environmental collapse would harm all communities"
- Social Justice Advocate evaluates Economist: "Market failures require government intervention"
- Social Justice Advocate evaluates Environmental Scientist: "Environment policy must consider vulnerable populations"

### Round 3: Refinement and Response
- Each expert responds to criticisms and adjusts their position
- Identification of common ground or shared concerns
- Exploration of potential compromises

### Round 4: Iterative Convergence
- Development of synthesised approach combining best elements
- Final rating of converged solution
- Identification of agreed elements

---

## ANALYSIS REQUIREMENTS

1. **Detailed Expert Positions:** Each expert must provide specific, evidence-based arguments
2. **Numerical Ratings:** All positions must be rated on 1-10 scale
3. **Cross-Evaluation:** Experts must evaluate and critique each other's positions
4. **Iterative Process:** Multiple rounds of refinement and synthesis
5. **Synthesis:** Final converged solution combining best elements

---

## EXPECTED OUTPUT

Provide a detailed analysis of the debate process including:
- Each expert's initial position with evidence
- Cross-criticism between experts
- Expert responses to criticism
- Final synthesized solution
- Overall rating of the consensus position
- List of agreed elements

**All output must be in English.**
